Introduction to Classes

Classes in Dungeons & Dragons help describe your character's abilities, strengths, and weaknesses.

Important: Once you choose a class you do not have to stick to it. You can mix and match different class abilities through multi-classing.

D&D Classes include:

  • Artificer
  • Barbarian
  • Bard
  • Cleric
  • Druid
  • Fighter
  • Monk
  • Paladin
  • Ranger
  • Rogue
  • Sorcerer
  • Warlock
  • Wizard

Note: Every class has several sub-classes you can choose at level 3 to further enhance your abilities within a specific field.

Classes Ranked

This comprehensive guide explains the best classes to play in D&D

  1. Barbarian
  2. Wizard
  3. Bard
  4. Druid

Barbarian is the simplest of the simple. While in battle, your trusty sword will never let you down and any handy-dandy magical teammates can buff you to shwo off your strong muscles.

Wizard falls on the opposite side of the spectrum. They use their extensive knowledge to win battles with their wits rather than their fists by slinging catastrophic spells all around the battlefield.

Bard doesn't care about strength or wits, but rather the musci of their instruments. Perfect for the player who loves to talk and explore the city.

Druid focuses on the connection with the wild rather than the social life of the city like the bard. They specialize in magic and become powerful when in their element.


This is a comprehensive description of the best D&D classes without any form of bias and is completely true.
